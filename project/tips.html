Inspection Guide

Check the Right Things Before You Buy

A smart used car purchase starts with a careful inspection. Review the vehicle’s condition, test its features, and pay attention to warning signs before making a final decision.

Exterior

Inspect the body, paint, glass, mirrors, lights, and tire wear.

  • Look for dents, rust, and paint mismatch.
  • Check headlights, taillights, and mirrors.
  • Inspect tire condition and uneven wear.

Interior

Make sure the cabin systems work properly and look for hidden issues.

  • Test windows, locks, seat belts, and A/C.
  • Check dashboard warning lights.
  • Look for water damage or bad smells.

Engine Area

Pay close attention to leaks, noise, and visible wear.

  • Check for leaks under the vehicle.
  • Listen for unusual sounds at idle.
  • Inspect belts, hoses, and battery condition.

Test Drive

Drive the vehicle calmly and notice how it responds.

  • Pay attention to braking, steering, and acceleration.
  • Listen for suspension noise.
  • Make sure the transmission shifts smoothly.

Documents

Paperwork is just as important as mechanical condition.

  • Check title status and VIN match.
  • Ask for maintenance records.
  • Verify emissions and registration information.

Red Flags

These signs may point to hidden problems or higher future costs.
